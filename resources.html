Resources for Information on U.S. Government and Politics

The White House Official Website: Provides information about the current administration, policies, and initiatives. https://www.whitehouse.gov/

United States Senate: Official website offering resources on senators, legislation, and committee activities. https://www.senate.gov/

United States House of Representatives: Official website with information on representatives, legislative activities, and committees. https://www.house.gov/

Supreme Court of the United States: Official website offering information on justices, cases, and rulings. https://www.supremecourt.gov/

The Library of Congress: Provides access to historical documents, including the Constitution, Declaration of Independence, and congressional records. https://www.loc.gov/

National Archives: Offers access to primary sources, historical documents, and exhibits related to American history and government. https://www.archives.gov/

C-SPAN: Provides live coverage and archives of congressional proceedings, speeches, and events. https://www.c-span.org/

The Federalist Papers: Collection of essays written by Alexander Hamilton, James Madison, and John Jay, advocating for the ratification of the Constitution. https://guides.loc.gov/federalist-papers/full-text

Congressional Research Service (CRS): Provides nonpartisan research and analysis for Congress on various legislative issues. https://crsreports.congress.gov/

Pew Research Center: Conducts public opinion polling and research on political attitudes, trends, and demographics. https://www.pewresearch.org/

American Political Science Association (APSA): Offers resources for political science research, teaching, and professional development. https://www.apsanet.org/About/About-APSA

Project Vote Smart: Nonprofit organization providing information on candidates, elections, and political issues. https://justfacts.votesmart.org/

Ballotpedia: Provides information on elections, candidates, and ballot measures at the federal, state, and local levels. https://ballotpedia.org/Main_Page

Brookings Institution: Think tank conducting research and analysis on public policy issues, including governance, democracy, and political reform. https://www.brookings.edu/

Heritage Foundation: Conservative think tank offering research and analysis on policy issues, including government reform and constitutional principles. https://www.heritage.org/

Center for American Progress: A think tank conducting research and advocacy on a range of policy issues, including government accountability and transparency. https://www.americanprogress.org/

Council on Foreign Relations: Provides analysis and resources on U.S. foreign policy, international relations, and global governance. https://www.cfr.org/

National Conference of State Legislatures (NCSL): Offers resources and research on state-level government, legislation, and policy issues. https://www.ncsl.org/

Congressional Budget Office (CBO): Provides nonpartisan analysis of budgetary and economic issues for Congress. https://www.cbo.gov/

The Avalon Project - Documents in Law, History, and Diplomacy. https://avalon.law.yale.edu/subject_menus/18th.asp

Mount Vernon - George Washington's Mount Vernon: https://www.mountvernon.org/

The Papers of Thomas Jefferson: https://jeffersonpapers.princeton.edu/

James Madison's Montpelier: https://www.montpelier.org/

USA.gov: Official website of the U.S. government, offering resources and information on federal agencies, programs, and services. https://www.usa.gov/

Center for Responsive Politics (OpenSecrets.org): Tracks money in U.S. politics, including campaign contributions, lobbying expenditures, and financial disclosures. https://www.opensecrets.org/

FactCheck.org: Nonpartisan fact-checking website that monitors the accuracy of statements made by politicians and public figures. https://www.factcheck.org/

National Public Radio (NPR) Politics Section: Offers news coverage, analysis, and podcasts on U.S. politics and government. https://www.npr.org/

The Atlantic: Publishes articles, essays, and analysis on politics, culture, and society, including historical perspectives on American governance. https://www.theatlantic.com/

The Washington Post Politics Section: Offers news, analysis, and commentary on current political events and developments. https://www.washingtonpost.com/politics/

Politico: Covers political news, analysis, and commentary on Capitol Hill and beyond. https://www.politico.com/
